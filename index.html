Arts and Crafts

I love creating paper decorations for my apartment. I first got into it back when I lived in the dorms, making paper door decorations for every holiday because I wanted to spread a little extra cheer to my neighbors. Since then, paper crafting has become one of my favorite ways to personalize my space. Alongside that, I really enjoy working on paint-by-numbers pieces, they’re calming, almost meditative, and a great way for me to unwind after a long day. Recently, I also picked up cross-stitch embroidery. It’s slow, repetitive, and surprisingly satisfying, and I’ve found it to be another soothing way to de-stress while still being creative

Hiking & Outdoors

I grew up in Scouting, so the outdoors naturally became a second home for me. From a young age, I spent countless hours hiking, camping, and learning how to navigate trails, and those experiences shaped the way I connect with the world. Being outside has always felt grounding, there’s something incredibly peaceful about stepping away from busy routines and surrounding myself with trees, open air, and quiet. Going on hikes or simply exploring a new patch of forest gives me space to clear my head, reset my thoughts, and reconnect with myself. Whether it’s a long trail or a short walk, spending time in nature has become one of the most dependable ways for me to find calm and regain balance in my life.

Photography

I started taking photos at a young age as a way to express my creativity, and it quickly became one of my favorite forms of art. When I’m out on hikes or exploring new places, I love capturing the natural world, the textures, the colors, and the small moments you only notice when you slow down. Recently, I’ve also begun photographing my Cross Country and Track & Field teams. Those photos usually end up on the teams’ Instagram pages, and while they’re simple, I enjoy the energy and connection that comes from documenting my teammates in action. Sports photography is fun and fast-paced, and it feels good to contribute something creative to the teams I’m a part of. At the same time, nature photography still holds a special place for me. It gives me a way to show others the beauty I see around me and to preserve the moments that make the outdoors so meaningful. I always try to capture scenes as I experience them, doing my best to translate what I’m feeling into an image.
